Description

Subject that studies the mathematical foundations of information processing, i.e. how to compress an information source with bits and how to send these bits through a digital communications channel. It is highly based on concepts of probability, is given in the third term of the second year and is common to the degrees of telecommunication networks and audiovisual systems. Occasionally, students of the computer science degree or the biomedical degree have taken it as an elective.

The subject is structured in 9 subjects with a teaching load corresponding to 5 ECTS credits, that is to say, a total of 125 hours of dedication by the student, of which 46 are face-to-face. The main learning methods of the subject are problem-based learning (PBL), gamification (G), flipped classroom (FC) and peer review (PR).

Annex 2. Example of evaluation criteria

Grading scale for content

  • 1–2: poor in content and depth of understanding
  • 3–4: important information is missing, tiptoes around the main concepts
  • 5–6: adequate in content and understanding, a simple story is built
  • 7–8: further details are provided, relates and connects several concepts, more elaborated flow
  • 9–10: goes beyond the content explained in class, further conclusions and implications of the study

Grading scale for presentation

  • 1–2: absence of sentences, many mixed formats and no structure
  • 3–4: no paragraphs with spelling and grammar flaws, poor format and almost no structure
  • 5–6: basic use of the language, minimal basic structure
  • 7–8: correct use of grammar to present the concepts, well structured, could be improved
  • 9–10: excellent use of scientific language, very good in structure and text, equations and figures format
