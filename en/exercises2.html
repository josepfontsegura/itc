Exercises: 2. Functions of random variables and concentration inequalities

Exercise 2.1. Which of the following is correct for $\mathbb{E}[X]$ and $\mathbb{E}[Y]$?

(a) $\mathbb{E}[X] = \tfrac{7}{10}$ and $\mathbb{E}[Y] = \tfrac{2}{5}$
(b) $\mathbb{E}[X] = \tfrac{2}{5}$ and $\mathbb{E}[Y] = \tfrac{7}{10}$
(c) $\mathbb{E}[X] = \tfrac{7}{10}$ and $\mathbb{E}[Y] = \tfrac{3}{5}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

By normalization, we deduce that $P_{XY}(1,1) = \tfrac{2}{5}$. Since $X$ and $Y$ are binary, $\mathbb{E}[X] = P_X(1)$ and $\mathbb{E}[Y] = P_Y(1)$. Using $ P_X(1) = P_{XY}(1,0) + P_{XY}(1,1) = \tfrac{3}{10} + \tfrac{2}{5} = \tfrac{7}{10} $ and $ P_Y(1) = P_{XY}(0,1) + P_{XY}(1,1) = \tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{2}{5} = \tfrac{3}{5}, $ we find that the correct answer.

Exercise 2.2. What is the alphabet and probability distribution of $Z$, respectively?

(a) $\{0,1\}$ and $\bigl\{\tfrac12, \tfrac12\bigr\}$
(b) $\{0,1,2\}$ and $\bigl\{\tfrac{1}{10}, \tfrac{1}{2}, \tfrac{2}{5}\bigr\}$
(c) $\{0,1\}$ and $\bigl\{\tfrac{2}{5}, \tfrac{3}{5}\bigr\}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Since $Z = 0$ when $X=Y$, that is $(0,0)$ or $(1,1)$, we get $ P_Z(0) = P_{XY}(0,0) + P_{XY}(1,1) = \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{2}{5} = \tfrac{1}{10} + \tfrac{4}{10} = \tfrac{5}{10} = \tfrac12. $ Also, $Z=1$ when $X\neq Y$, that is $(0,1)$ or $(1,0)$, hence $ P_Z(1) = \tfrac{1}{5} + \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{2}{10} + \tfrac{3}{10} = \tfrac{5}{10} = \tfrac12. $ Therefore, $Z\in\{0,1\}$ with equal probability.

Exercise 2.3. What is the value of $\mathbb{E}[\log_2(1+Z)]$?

(a) $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(b) $\tfrac{13}{10}$
(c) $1+\log_2(3)$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Since $Z$ is 0 or 1 equiprobably, $\mathbb{E}[\log_2(1+Z)] = \log_2(1)\cdot\tfrac12 + \log_2(2)\cdot\tfrac12 = \tfrac12.$

Exercise 2.4. What is the value of $\text{var}(Z\oplus Z)$?

(a) $\tfrac{41}{100}$
(b) $\tfrac{1}{4}$
(c) $0$

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

Since $Z\oplus Z = 0$ with probability one, its variance is zero.

Exercise 2.5. For $n=4$, which of the following corresponds to the exact probability $\mathbb{P}\bigl[Y\geq \tfrac34 \bigr]$ and to the value of the bound given by Markov's inequality, respectively?

(a) $\tfrac{5}{16}$ and $\tfrac{2}{3}$
(b) $\tfrac{1}{4}$ and $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(c) $\tfrac{1}{16}$ and $\tfrac{3}{4}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

For $n=4$, we have $$ \mathbb{P}\bigl[Y\geq \tfrac34 \bigr] = P_Y\bigl(\tfrac 34\bigr) + P_Y\bigl(1) = \tfrac{4}{16}+\tfrac{1}{16} = \tfrac{5}{16}. $$ On the other hand, Markov's inequality states $$ \mathbb{P}[Y \ge a] \le \dfrac{\mathbb{E}[Y]}{a}. $$ Here, $\mathbb{E}[Y]=\tfrac12$ and $a=\tfrac34$, leading to the correct answer.

Exercise 2.6. For $n=4$, which of the following corresponds to the exact probability $\mathbb{P}\bigl[|Y-\mathbb{E}[Y]|\ge \tfrac12 \bigr]$ and to the value of the bound given by Chebyshev's inequality, respectively?

(a) $\tfrac{1}{8}$ and $\tfrac{1}{4}$
(b) $\tfrac{3}{8}$ and $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(c) $\tfrac{1}{4}$ and $\tfrac{3}{8}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Since $\mathbb{E}[Y]=\tfrac 12$, let us define $Z = |Y - \mathbb{E}[Y]| = |\,Y - \tfrac12|$. We observe that $Y - \tfrac12$ takes values in $\{-\tfrac12, -\tfrac14, 0,\tfrac14, \tfrac12\}$ with probabilities $\{\tfrac{1}{16},\tfrac{4}{16},\tfrac{6}{16},\tfrac{4}{16},\tfrac{1}{16}\}$. As a consequence, $Z$ takes value sin $\{0,\tfrac14,\tfrac12\}$ with probabilities $\{\tfrac{1}{16},\tfrac{8}{16},\tfrac{2}{16}\}$. Hence, $$ \mathbb{P}[|Y - \mathbb{E}[Y]| \ge \tfrac12] = \mathbb{P}[Z \ge \tfrac12] = P_Z(\tfrac12) = \tfrac18. $$ On the other hand, Chebyshev's inequality for this probability reads $$ \mathbb{P}[|Y - \mathbb{E}[Y]| \ge \tfrac12] \le \frac{\text{var}(Y)}{\left(\tfrac12\right)^2}. $$ Since $$ \text{var}(Y) = \mathbb{E}[Y^2] - (\mathbb{E}[Y])^2 = \tfrac{5}{16} - \Bigl(\tfrac12\Bigr)^2 = \tfrac{1}{16}, $$ we obtain $$ \mathbb{P}[|Y - \tfrac12| \ge \tfrac12] \le \frac{\tfrac{1}{16}}{\tfrac14} = \tfrac14. $$

Exercise 2.7. Which of the following statements is true? You can reason by elimination.

(a) $|\mathcal{U}| = 2^n$
(b) $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{g(2^n)}{P_U(2^n)} = -\infty$
(c) $\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} n P_U(u) = 0$

Show solution

The correct answer is (b) ★

$|\mathcal{U}| = n$ and $P_U(u) = 1/n$ for all $u$, so that $\lim_{n\to\infty} n P_U(u) = 1$. Also, $g(2^n)/P_U(2^n) = (-n/2)/(1/n) = -n^2/2$ so that $\lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{g(2^n)}{P_U(2^n)} = -\infty$.

Exercise 2.8. Find the formula for the sum of natural numbers between $1$ and $n$ and use the result to find the mean of $g(U)$, $\mathrm{E}[g(U)]$. If you use ChatGPT at any step, make sure the answer is correct.

(a) $-\frac{1}{4}n(n+1)$
(b) $-\frac{1}{4}(n+1)$
(c) $-\frac{1}{2}(n+1)$

Show solution

The correct answer is (b) ★

The sum of natural numbers between $1$ and $n$ is $\frac{n(n+1)}{2}$; since $g(u)$ has a minus sign, the mean of $g(u)$ is $-\frac{n+1}{4}$.

Exercise 2.9. Find the formula giving the sum of the squares of natural numbers between $1$ and $n$ and use the result to find the mean of $g^2(U)$, $\mathrm{E}[g^2(U)]$.

(a) $\frac{1}{4}(n+1)^2$
(b) $\frac{1}{12}(n+1)(n+\frac{1}{2})$
(c) $\frac{1}{6}(n+1)(2n+1)$

Show solution

The correct answer is (b) ★

The sum of the squares of natural numbers between $1$ and $n$ is $\frac{n(n+1)(2n+1)}{6}$; since $g(2^a)=-a/2$, the mean of $g^2(u)$ is $1/4$ times the sum of squares divided by $n$.

Exercise 2.10. For which value of $n$ is the variance of $g(U)$ equal to $1$?

(a) $1$
(b) $2$
(c) $7$

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

The variance can be calculated as $\frac{1}{48}(n^2-1)$, which is $1$ for $n=7$.

Exercise 2.11. What is the upper bound on the probability $\mathrm{Pr}\bigl[g(\mathbf{V}) \leq -n/2 \bigr]$ in Markov's inequality?

(a) It cannot be calculated
(b) $-\frac{1}{2}$
(c) $2$

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

$g(\mathbf{V})$ is negative and Markov's inequality upper bounds $\mathrm{Pr}\bigl[-g(\mathbf{V}) \geq n/2 \bigr]$ by $\frac{-\mathrm{E}[g(\mathbf{V})]}{n/2} = 2$.

Exercise 2.12. What is $\mathrm{Pr}\bigl[g(\mathbf{V}) \leq -n/2\bigr]$?

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

In fact, $g(\mathbf{V})$ is a constant random variable, with a value of $-n$, so the probability of it being less than $-n/2$ is one.

Exercise 2.15. A source $X$ taking values in $\{-1,0,1\}$ with equal probability is compressed using the optimal binary prefix-free code. The function $\ell(x)$ returns the length of the encoded version of the symbol $x\in\{-1,0,1\}$. What is the value of the Chebyshev's upper bound to the probability $\mathbb{P}[|\ell(X)-L_{\rm av}|\geq L_{\rm av}]$, where $L_{\rm av}$ is the average length of the code?

(a) $0$
(b) $\frac{2}{15}$
(c) $\frac{2}{25}$
(d) $\frac{27}{25}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

The probability $\mathbb{P}[|\ell(X)-L_{\rm av}|\geq L_{\rm av}]$ is of the form of Chebyshev's inequality since $\mathbb{E}[\ell(X)]=L_{\rm av}$, and is given by $\frac{{\rm Var}(\ell(X))}{L_{\rm av}^2}$. After constructing the optimal (Huffman) code for $X$, we find that $\ell(X)\in\{1,2,2\}$ with equal probability, leading to ${\rm Var}(\ell(X)) = \frac 29$ and $\mathbb{E}[\ell(X)]=\frac 53$.

Exercise 2.16. A source $X$ taking values in $\{-2,-1,1,2\}$ with equal probability is compressed using the optimal binary prefix-free code. For each symbol $x\in\{-2,-1,1,2\}$, the function $\ell(x)$ returns the length of its encoded version. What is the value of the probability $\mathbb{P}\bigr[|\ell(X)-L_{\rm av}|\geq \mathbb{E}[X]\bigr]$, where $L_{\rm av}$ is the average length of the code?

(a) $0$
(b) $\frac{2}{25}$
(c) $1$
(d) Indeterminate

Show solution

The correct answer is (c) ★

The optimal (Huffman) code is given by $\{00,01,10,11\}$, hence $\ell(x)=L_{\rm av}=2$. Since $\mathbb{E}[X]=0$, the probability is $1$.

Exercise 2.17. Let $X$ be a non-negative random variable with $\mathbb{E}[X]=4$. What does Markov's inequality say about $\mathbb{P}[X\geq 8]$?

(a) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq 8]\leq \tfrac{1}{2}$
(b) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq 8]\leq 2$
(c) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq 8]\leq \tfrac{1}{4}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Markov's inequality states $\mathbb{P}[X\geq a]\leq \tfrac{\mathbb{E}[X]}{a}=\tfrac{4}{8}=\tfrac{1}{2}$.

Exercise 2.18. Let $X$ have mean $\mu=3$ and variance $\sigma^2=1$. What is the Chebyshev bound on $\mathbb{P}[|X-3|\geq 2]$?

(a) $\tfrac{1}{4}$
(b) $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(c) $\tfrac{3}{4}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Chebyshev's inequality gives $\mathbb{P}[|X-\mu|\geq k]\leq \tfrac{\sigma^2}{k^2}=\tfrac{1}{4}$.

Exercise 2.19. Let $X_1,\ldots,X_n$ be i.i.d. with $\mathbb{E}[X_i]=\mu$ and $\text{var}(X_i)=\sigma^2$. What is $\text{var}\bigl(\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i\bigr)$?

(a) $\tfrac{\sigma^2}{n}$
(b) $\sigma^2$
(c) $n\sigma^2$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

By independence, $\text{var}\bigl(\tfrac{1}{n}\sum X_i\bigr)=\tfrac{1}{n^2}\sum \text{var}(X_i)=\tfrac{n\sigma^2}{n^2}=\tfrac{\sigma^2}{n}$.

Exercise 2.20. A fair die is rolled $n$ times and $Y=\tfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i$ is the sample mean. By the law of large numbers, what does $Y$ converge to as $n\to\infty$?

(a) $\tfrac{7}{2}$
(b) $3$
(c) $\tfrac{1}{6}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

The law of large numbers states that $Y$ converges in probability to $\mathbb{E}[X_i]=\tfrac{1+2+3+4+5+6}{6}=\tfrac{7}{2}$.

Exercise 2.21. Let $Z$ be a random variable with $\mathbb{E}[Z]=0$ and $\mathbb{E}[Z^2]=9$. Using Chebyshev's inequality, what is an upper bound on $\mathbb{P}[|Z|\geq 6]$?

(a) $\tfrac{1}{4}$
(b) $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(c) $\tfrac{3}{4}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

We have $\text{var}(Z)=\mathbb{E}[Z^2]-(\mathbb{E}[Z])^2=9$. Chebyshev gives $\mathbb{P}[|Z|\geq 6]\leq \tfrac{9}{36}=\tfrac{1}{4}$.

Exercise 2.22. Let $X$ be a non-negative random variable. Which of the following is a consequence of Markov's inequality?

(a) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq a]\leq \tfrac{\mathbb{E}[X]}{a}$ for all $a>0$
(b) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq a]\geq \tfrac{\mathbb{E}[X]}{a}$ for all $a>0$
(c) $\mathbb{P}[X\geq a]= \tfrac{\mathbb{E}[X]}{a}$ for all $a>0$

The correct answer is (a) ★

Markov's inequality provides an upper bound on the tail probability of a non-negative random variable.

Exercise 2.23. Let $X_1,\ldots,X_{100}$ be i.i.d. Bernoulli$(\tfrac{1}{2})$. Using Chebyshev's inequality, what is an upper bound on $\mathbb{P}\bigl[|\bar{X}-\tfrac{1}{2}|\geq \tfrac{1}{10}\bigr]$, where $\bar{X}=\tfrac{1}{100}\sum X_i$?

(a) $\tfrac{1}{4}$
(b) $\tfrac{1}{2}$
(c) $\tfrac{1}{100}$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

We have $\text{var}(\bar{X})=\tfrac{1}{400}$. Chebyshev gives $\mathbb{P}\bigl[|\bar{X}-\tfrac{1}{2}|\geq \tfrac{1}{10}\bigr]\leq \tfrac{1/400}{1/100}=\tfrac{1}{4}$.

Exercise 2.24. For a random variable $X$ with mean $\mu$, Chebyshev's inequality can be stated as $\mathbb{P}[|X-\mu|\geq k\sigma]\leq$

(a) $\tfrac{1}{k^2}$
(b) $\tfrac{\sigma}{k}$
(c) $k^2\sigma^2$

Show solution

The correct answer is (a) ★

Chebyshev's inequality in this form gives $\mathbb{P}[|X-\mu|\geq k\sigma]\leq \tfrac{\text{var}(X)}{k^2\sigma^2}=\tfrac{1}{k^2}$.
